Harvard College Marathon Challenge

Welcome to Harvard College Marathon Challenge (HCMC)!

We are an on-campus running group open to all regardless of running background or experience! Join our mailing list to get notified about runs, or post to the list yourself!

Harvard College Marathon Challenge (HCMC) is an organized group of Harvard students, staff, faculty, and alums that raises money through running to benefit the health, education, and welfare of Boston-area youth and families. Since its inception in 2005-06, HCMC runners have raised over $850,000 for causes such as the Phillips Brooks House Association (PBHA), Project HEALTH, and the American Medical Athletic Association (AMAA). This year, our runners will raise money for the Phillips Brooks House Association (PBHA), a student-run, Harvard-based charity that operates over 70 community-based programs in Greater Boston.

In 2022-23, by virtue of their fundraising efforts, our official HCMC participants will be among the lucky few to earn invitational charity entries to run the 127th Boston Marathon on Monday, April 17, 2023. Between now and then, participants will meet and train with other Harvard runners, including many who are not running in the Boston Marathon but who just want to run with other Harvard community members.
